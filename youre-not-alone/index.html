Meet your mentor

Here's someone who's been here before, and who can be there for you.

Marian studied photography, but ended up in a stressful job in marketing. She wanted to break free, and was prepared to make some big spending cuts to achieve her goal.

“I decided I needed to make a change. I read around to see how other people had escaped and came up with a plan. I spent a year preparing for the day when I was going to hand in my resignation. I moved back in with my parents and saved as much of my salary as I could. I stopped spending on anything that was not necessary - clothes, eating out, taxis. I joined my local library and cycled to work.”

Marian now earns £1500 a month in passive income

“I worked evenings and weekends for a year. Every penny went into my investment account. It was tough but it was worth it. Now the income from the money I saved gives me enough to live on while I get started as a photographer. I still live frugally - and I love it! I’m in control of my life and my future.”

Chris was always out of cash by the end of the month, and the amount he owed wasn’t going down. With credit card debts to clear and a student loan, money was always a source of worry.

“I had a moment when I realised I’d never have the things my parents did - a home, a family, a fun retirement. And I couldn’t understand why. I started going back through my statements and seeing what I was spending money on. Even though I was always stressing about money, I was blowing it on things to make me less stressed - the pub after work, eating out, bars and gigs.”

Chris now earns £1500 a month in passive income

“I started setting a budget. Now I have a bit of money at the end of the month which is great. Some goes towards paying off debts, and some I invest which pays me a small income. When I think about money now, I’m much more relaxed. I’m not there yet, but I’m getting there.”

Marie was finally earning the salary she wanted. She was able to afford a lot more stuff, but she was still stressed out.

"Working full time was taking its toll. I had to work out what was really important to me. I used to think new clothes every month made me happy. I used to think the gym was the only option, and that the stress of my job meant I deserved all those little treats. But the reality was that these things were a trap. I was working to live and living to work.

Marie now earns £1500 a month in passive income

“Now I run instead of paying for the gym and make a pack lunch instead of eating in a cafe. These 2 simple changes save me thousands of pounds a year. I’ve cut out a lot of other expenses, and I don’t miss them. I invest the money I save and the income from that means I now work 4 days a week. In 5 years I’ll work 2 days a week, so I can spend more time volunteering, which I love.”

Mark was leaving for work before his children woke up. When an old friend asked him to be his best man, Mark realised they'd not seen each other for 4 years. How did this happen?

“I needed to make time for the people in my life who are important to me - which meant working less. I was able to go down to 4 days a week, but we really noticed the drop in income. We had to save, and I couldn’t see how. Where did our money go? A quick look around our kids' bedrooms told us the answer; expensive toys, clothes and gadgets.”

Mark now earns £1500 a month in passive income

“We had an honest discussion about spending. The kids were great - they went through all their things and made a huge pile of stuff to sell on eBay. Now invest the money we save in stocks and shares ISAs for their future. We buy stuff second hand now - or do without. I get to spend more time with the family, and you can't put a price on that.”
